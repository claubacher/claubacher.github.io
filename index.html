About me

Chris Lau·ba·cher /ˈlɑːbɑːkər/, n.

In 2010, I graduated from the University of Chicago with a degree in English and Film Studies. Wait. English? Film? I thought you said you were a developer. Let me explain.

My working years began with a desk job at an education company. As great of an opportunity as that was, I found myself getting a little bored. There was this part of my brain that just wasn't being used to its fullest extent. So I started gravitating toward projects that were a little more technical, projects that had me fixing bugs in XML templates or constructing formulas in Excel. And I realized: I like this kind of thing. I like using technology to solve problems.

I started thinking seriously about the kind of work I wanted to be doing. And I started reading. I started reading wonderful books like Gödel, Escher, Bach and Turing's Cathedral, books that not only introduced me to what programming was but also inspired me to give it a try. I started taking some of those newfangled MOOCs – courses like Udacity's Intro to Computer Science and Coursera's Machine Learning. At last! Here was the intellectual stimulation I was craving. I knew I was on the right track.

So, I decided to take the plunge. I signed up for Dev Bootcamp, an intensive 9-week coding program in Chicago. Each week, I spent 70-100 hours tackling coding challenges and building apps. I learned the fundamentals of Ruby; databases and SQL; HTML, CSS, and JavaScript (including jQuery); Git; test-driven development and RSpec; Sinatra; and Rails. I also became a whole lot better at giving and receiving feedback, asking questions, and working as part of a team.

Now I'm looking for a new opportunity to keep learning and keep honing my craft.
